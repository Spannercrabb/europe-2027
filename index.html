💡 Fantastic location! Zähringerstrasse is right in the heart of Zurich's Altstadt (Old Town) — you can walk to Grossmünster, the lake, Bahnhofstrasse shopping, and countless cafés in just minutes. Zurich Main Station (for the Basel train on Saturday) is also only a short walk away. Perfect base for your birthday celebration! 🎂

Getting from the airport

  • Zurich Airport (ZRH) to city centre is easy — trains run every 10 minutes and take about 12 minutes to Zurich Hauptbahnhof (main station).
  • A taxi is around CHF 60–70.

Things to see & do

  • Altstadt (Old Town) — wander the medieval laneways, peek into Grossmünster and Fraumünster churches
  • Bahnhofstrasse — one of the world's most elegant shopping streets, perfect for window browsing
  • Lake Zurich boat trip — a short scenic ride with mountain views
  • Uetliberg Mountain — 20-minute train ride, then an easy walk to the top for panoramic Alps views
  • Lindenhof — quiet hilltop park built on Roman foundations, free lookout over the old town

🏛️ History highlights

  • Swiss National Museum — right by the station, 1000 years of Swiss history under one roof. Free audio guide in English, fabulous for a rainy afternoon. Allow 2–3 hours.
  • Grossmünster Cathedral — where the Swiss-German Reformation began in the 1500s (Huldrych Zwingli preached here). Climb the tower for epic views over the lake.
  • Fraumünster Church — famous for its Marc Chagall stained-glass windows (1970) — absolutely magical on a sunny morning. Small entry fee, worth every cent.
  • Lindenhof hill — this was the site of a Roman customs house 2,000 years ago. Now it's a peaceful park with huge chess boards and the best free view in the city.
  • St. Peter's Church — has the largest clock face in Europe (8.6 metres wide!) — right in the old town.
  • Kunsthaus Zurich — Switzerland's top art museum, with Picasso, Monet, Van Gogh, and Swiss masters. Recently expanded.

🥖 Food markets & food streets

  • Markthalle im Viadukt 🏆 — a gorgeous covered food hall in a converted railway viaduct. Artisan cheese, fresh bread, Swiss wine, charcuterie, olives, flowers. Open Mon–Sat. A must-visit!
  • Bürkliplatz Farmer's Market — right on the lake! Tuesday & Friday mornings, local produce, cheese, flowers, honey. Beautiful lakeside setting.
  • Helvetiaplatz Market — local produce market, Tuesday & Friday mornings in the trendy Kreis 4 district
  • Niederdorfstrasse — the old town's food & restaurant street, great for evening strolls with gelato or hot chocolate
  • Confiserie Sprüngli (Bahnhofstrasse) — legendary 1836 chocolate shop. Pick up Luxemburgerli (tiny macarons) and truffles. Upstairs café is a treat!
  • Schober (Napfgasse) — famous old-world café for real Swiss hot chocolate and pastries

Food & treats to try

  • Cheese fondue at a traditional restaurant — classic Swiss comfort food
  • Rösti — crispy Swiss potato pancake, often topped with eggs or cheese
  • Luxemburgerli from Confiserie Sprüngli — tiny macarons, take a box home
  • Hot chocolate at any Sprüngli or Lindt café
💡 Tip: Zurich is expensive — about 1.5× Australian prices. A simple café lunch is AUD $30–40. Use supermarkets (Coop, Migros) for cheaper snacks and picnic food.

🏛️ What to do in one afternoon — history highlights

🌲 The Black Forest INCLUDED
Day 2 · Breisach, Germany · Monday 29 March · 🕗 8:15 AM · ⏱️ 4 hours · 🥾 Easy · 📸 Sightseeing

Admire the dense, lofty fir forests of Germany's Schwarzwald — the fabled Black Forest. You'll set off with your guide on a scenic drive through a mythical land of cuckoo clocks, glassblowing and timeless fairy tales, winding through wine-producing villages cradled among soft, undulating hills and vineyards. The journey climbs up to the beautiful Hofgut Sternen, a loyal keeper of Black Forest traditions, where you can choose how to soak it all up — an invigorating forest stroll, a cuckoo-clock demonstration, watching a glassblower at work, or even learning to make the decadent Black Forest Cake the region is famous for. Afterwards you'll return to the ship via Freiburg, a major university and cathedral city.

🥾 Easy — walking is mostly over even surfaces with few steps or inclines; total time walking or standing is normally no more than about 1.5 hours.
⛪ Strasbourg Highlights INCLUDED
Day 3 · Strasbourg, France · Tuesday 30 March · 🕗 8:45 AM · ⏱️ 4 hours · 🥾 Moderate · 🛍️ Shopping · 📸 Sightseeing

Get to know this delightful city steeped in equal parts French and German culture. You'll drive through the German Imperial District and the European Quarter — home to the European Union and the Council of Europe — passing remarkable Renaissance architecture on the way to the city centre. From there, enjoy a walk through picturesque Petite France, the former Tanners' District wrapped by the Ill River and crisscrossed by charming canals, where you'll see the Old Customs House and the bridges with their defensive towers. In Cathedral Square, admire the magnificently restored Strasbourg Cathedral — one of Europe's finest Gothic cathedrals and the tallest building in the world from 1647 to 1874. There's free time to take in its great Gothic rosette window, red sandstone portal and remarkable astronomical clock.

🥾 Moderate — walking portions may be long and somewhat challenging, with occasional step-ups, stairs and inclines; total time walking or standing normally ranges from 1.5 to 3 hours.
🏰 Speyer Walking Tour INCLUDED
Day 4 · Speyer, Germany · Wednesday 31 March · 🕗 8:45 AM · ⏱️ 3 hours · 🥾 Moderate · 🏛️ UNESCO · 📸 Sightseeing

Enjoy a fascinating stroll through Speyer, home to the largest of the three Romanesque imperial cathedrals. It's believed the Protestants earned their name here in 1529, after citizens and royalty drafted the "Protestation at Speyer," proclaiming their right to practise Christianity as they pleased. During your walk you'll marvel at the exterior of the red sandstone cathedral, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, where eight emperors are entombed — including some of the most notable Holy Roman Emperors — its six towers dominating the skyline after being rebuilt following Louis XIV's 1689 invasion. You'll pass the Jewish courtyard, once home to one of the most important medieval synagogues, and Speyer's Altpörtel (Old Gate), the last remains of the town's fortifications. There's also time to stroll the charming, shop-lined main street.

🥾 Moderate — walking portions may be long and somewhat challenging, with occasional step-ups, stairs and inclines; total time walking or standing normally ranges from 1.5 to 3 hours.
🐎 Historic Koblenz INCLUDED
Day 5 · Koblenz, Germany · Thursday 1 April · 🕑 2:00 PM · ⏱️ 2 hours · 🥾 Moderate · 🛍️ Shopping · 📸 Sightseeing

Take a leisurely stroll around Koblenz and explore its historic past. You'll meet your guide at Deutsches Eck, the city's famed "German Corner" — jutting into the water like the prow of a ship where the Rhine and Moselle meet, this public park is home to a grand equestrian statue of Emperor Wilhelm I. After time to admire the sculpture, you'll continue among the narrow streets to Jesuitenplatz (Jesuit Square) in the heart of the Old Town, where the beautiful old school on the eastern side has served as Koblenz City Hall since 1895. As you explore, your guide will point out influences from ancient Rome, the Middle Ages and the Napoleonic era.

🥾 Moderate — walking portions may be long and somewhat challenging, with occasional step-ups, stairs and inclines; total time walking or standing normally ranges from 1.5 to 3 hours.
⛪ Cologne Walking Tour INCLUDED
Day 6 · Cologne, Germany · Friday 2 April · 🕘 9:15 AM · ⏱️ 2 hours · 🥾 Moderate · 🏛️ UNESCO · 📸 Sightseeing · 🛍️ Shopping

Learn about Cologne's heritage and view its iconic cathedral, towering over the city in all its Gothic splendour. You'll follow the city's preserved historic street patterns, hearing about its Roman and medieval past, while your guide shares tales of local folklore figures Tünnes and Schääl and the city's flourishing Jewish community. In Old Cologne you'll pass the elegant silhouette of St. Martin's church and stop to admire Cologne's magnificent Gothic cathedral, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Begun in 1248 and completed in stages over the next seven centuries, it's the largest Gothic cathedral in Northern Europe — two soaring spires, beautiful stained-glass windows, and a survivor of the Allied bombings that ravaged the city during World War II.

🥾 Moderate — walking portions may be long and somewhat challenging, with occasional step-ups, stairs and inclines; total time walking or standing normally ranges from 1.5 to 3 hours.
🌬️ Kinderdijk Windmills INCLUDED
Day 7 · Kinderdijk, The Netherlands · Saturday 3 April · 🕟 4:30 PM · ⏱️ 2 hours · 🥾 Moderate · 🏛️ UNESCO · ⚙️ Working World

See the picturesque Dutch countryside and the original technological marvels of historic Kinderdijk. You'll walk with your guide into the serene polder lands of South Holland, crossing the dike to the windmills — which sit at a lower elevation than your ship, since much of this part of the country is below sea level. Built in the mid-18th century, these 19 windmills — the largest concentration in the Netherlands — stand like sentinels on a hushed landscape. You'll learn why they were built and see firsthand how they work, even stepping inside a working windmill to see its mechanics and living quarters (the families who live in them are required to keep them running). A revealing look at the marvellous buildings that helped shape the legendary Dutch landscape.

🥾 Moderate — walking portions may be long and somewhat challenging, with occasional step-ups, stairs and inclines; total time walking or standing normally ranges from 1.5 to 3 hours.

🚕 Getting to the station — taxi (easiest with luggage)

  • Best option with four of you and suitcases — book a larger taxi (estate car or van)
  • TCA (Taxicentrale Amsterdam) — the official, largest & most trusted taxi company; English-speaking dispatch, available 24/7
  • 📞 +31 20 777 7777 ("7 times 7" — easy to remember!)
  • When booking, just say "four passengers with four large suitcases" so they send a bigger vehicle
  • 💷 Approx cost: €15–20 for the whole car (about AUD $25–33) — roughly AUD $6–8 each. Prices are government-regulated, so no overcharging.
  • 💳 Pay by credit or debit card right in the taxi — no cash needed
  • Only about a 5–10 minute ride to Centraal
  • App alternatives: Bolt (usually cheapest) or Uber — choose "Uber XL" for the bigger car with luggage room
💡 Tip: Book the taxi the evening before, or ask the Linden Hotel reception to call one for you on the morning — they do this all the time and it takes the worry out of it. 😊

🌷 Tulips & flowers (the best time to visit is right NOW!)

  • Keukenhof Gardens 🏆 — the world's most famous tulip gardens, only open March–May. 7 million flowers across 32 hectares in every colour imaginable. A must on a trip to Holland in April! Day trip by bus or guided tour from Amsterdam (~45 minutes).
  • Flower fields by bike or tour — the tulip fields around Lisse (near Keukenhof) explode into colour in April. You can rent bikes or join a "tulip field" tour to see them from above.
  • Bloemenmarkt (floating flower market) — the world's only floating flower market, right in central Amsterdam on the Singel canal. Hundreds of fresh tulip bouquets and bulbs to take home (get the certified "export-ready" bulbs so customs won't confiscate them!)
  • Tulip Museum (Amsterdam Tulip Museum) — small but lovely, tells the whole history of "tulip mania" in the 1600s when a single bulb cost more than a house!
  • Hortus Botanicus — beautiful 1638 botanical gardens in the heart of Amsterdam, one of the oldest in the world. Stunning tulip displays in April.
  • FloraHolland Aalsmeer flower auction — the world's biggest flower trading floor. Go early (arrive by 7 am!) to see millions of flowers changing hands in real time. Unforgettable if you love flowers.

🏛️ History & culture

  • Anne Frank House — deeply moving, a must-visit. Book online at least 6 weeks in advance — they sell out fast and there's no ticket booth on the day.
  • Rijksmuseum — the big one! Vermeer's Milkmaid, Rembrandt's Night Watch, Dutch Golden Age masters. In a stunning 1885 building — allow 3 hours.
  • Van Gogh Museum — the world's largest Van Gogh collection (200+ paintings). Incredible to see the colours in real life. Pre-book tickets a few weeks ahead.
  • Canal cruise — a gentle 1-hour boat tour is the loveliest way to see the city. Book a "small boat" tour (max 12 people) for a better experience than the big glass boats.
  • Begijnhof — hidden medieval courtyard from the 1300s, where Catholic women lived quietly. Totally peaceful and free to visit.
  • Dam Square & Royal Palace — the heart of Amsterdam, with the 17th-century palace where Dutch royals still occasionally live
  • Westerkerk — the tallest church tower in Amsterdam, next to Anne Frank House. You can climb it for city views (mild stairs!)
  • Jordaan neighbourhood — pretty canals, little cafés, vintage shops. The most photogenic area in the city.
  • Vondelpark — lovely park for a relaxed afternoon walk under blossom trees in April

🏛️ History highlights (Paris is a city of history!)

  • Louvre Museum 🏆 — the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and 35,000 treasures. Go early (9 am) or late afternoon to avoid crowds. Pre-book a timed entry. Don't try to see everything — pick 2–3 wings.
  • Notre-Dame Cathedral — reopened in December 2024 after the 2019 fire restoration. Free to enter. Gorgeous from the outside and absolutely stunning inside with its restored ceilings.
  • Sainte-Chapelle — the most beautiful stained-glass chapel in the world (1248). 15 massive stained-glass windows telling the whole Bible. Tucked on Île de la Cité — pre-book to skip the queue.
  • Musée d'Orsay — Monet, Renoir, Degas, Van Gogh, Cézanne. In a breathtaking former railway station from 1900. Many people prefer this to the Louvre!
  • Palace of Versailles — 45 minutes by train from central Paris. The Hall of Mirrors, Marie Antoinette's hamlet, vast gardens. Allow a full day and pre-book tickets!
  • Musée Carnavalet — the history of Paris itself, free entry! Room after room of Paris history from Roman times to today. A local gem.
  • Panthéon — final resting place of Voltaire, Rousseau, Marie Curie, Victor Hugo. Stunning Latin Quarter monument.
  • Conciergerie — the medieval palace that became a prison during the French Revolution. Marie Antoinette was held here before her execution. Genuinely moving.
  • Arc de Triomphe — climb the 284 steps for a view straight down the Champs-Élysées
  • Musée de l'Orangerie — Monet's huge Water Lilies in oval rooms. Small, quiet, and breathtaking.

🥖 Food markets & food streets (Paris is heaven for this!)

What you need as an Australian
Apply: early 2027

Good news: Australians do not need a traditional visa for any of the countries on your trip! Switzerland, Germany, the Netherlands, and France are all in the Schengen Area, where Australians can visit visa-free for up to 90 days.

🆕 ETIAS — the new travel authorisation

From late 2026, Europe is bringing in a new system called ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorisation System). It's not a visa — it's a quick online authorisation like the US ESTA. By March 2027, it's very likely to be required.

📋 What you'll need to apply

  • Email address
  • Debit or credit card for the €20 fee
  • Personal details (name, DOB, passport number)
  • Your first Schengen country of entry (that's Switzerland for you)

⏰ When to apply

  • As soon as ETIAS opens (expected late 2026)
  • Ideally by January or February 2027 — at least 4–6 weeks before flying
  • Don't leave it to the last minute! Approval is usually quick, but if there's any issue it can take up to 96 hours, and you cannot board your flight without it once the system is live

📘 Passport check — do this NOW

⭐ Important: Your passport must be valid for at least 3 months after 13 April 2027 — so valid until at least July 2027. If it expires before then, renew it now! Australian passport renewals can take 6+ weeks during busy periods. Apply at passports.gov.au.

🛂 Other things to know

  • EES (Entry/Exit System): A new biometric border system launched in 2025 — no application, just fingerprints/photo at the airport on arrival. Nothing to do beforehand.
  • 90-day rule: You can stay up to 90 days in any 180-day period across all Schengen countries combined — your 21-day trip is well within this
  • Switzerland is Schengen too (even though it's not in the EU) — you don't need anything extra for it
  • Hong Kong stopovers — nothing needed: You're only changing planes in Hong Kong (not leaving the airport), so no visa or paperwork is required for your stops. Australians get visa-free entry to Hong Kong anyway.

🚨 Australian Customs Warnings

Australia has strict biosecurity — here's what you need to know:

✅ Usually fine (but declare if asked):

  • Commercially packaged chocolate, biscuits, sweets
  • Sealed cheese, salami, vacuum-packed meats — declare at customs
  • Certified tulip bulbs (with export certificate)
  • Wine (2.25 litres per adult duty-free)

⚠️ MUST DECLARE (or risk a $3,000+ fine!):

  • Any food item — even in original packaging
  • Wooden items — cowbells, clogs, Christmas decorations, cuckoo clocks
  • Dried flowers, seeds, bulbs — must have certification
  • Animal products — leather, wool

❌ BANNED — don't even try:

  • 🍯 Honey of any kind (Australia has strict beekeeping laws)
  • 🥚 Fresh eggs or raw egg products
  • 🍎 Fresh fruit, vegetables, flowers
  • 🌱 Seeds, plants, soil
  • 🥩 Fresh meat (not sealed/canned)
💡 The golden rule: When in doubt, DECLARE it. Declaring is free. Getting caught with undeclared items is a $3,000+ fine and you can be refused entry. The customs form has one question: "Are you bringing in any food or animal/plant items?" — tick YES and let them check. They're usually friendly and will wave you through if the items are fine.

🗺️ Maps — download BEFORE you go!

This is one of the most useful tips — download offline maps of every city you're visiting. Then even if you run out of data or have no signal, Google Maps still works perfectly!

  1. Open Google Maps on your phone (at home, on WiFi)
  2. Search for Zurich (then Basel, Amsterdam, Paris, etc.)
  3. Tap the city name at the bottom
  4. Tap "Download" (or the three dots → "Download offline map")
  5. Repeat for every city on your itinerary

Also download these offline maps for the cruise stops:

  • Breisach & Colmar area (France/Germany border)
  • Strasbourg
  • Speyer, Rüdesheim, Koblenz
  • Cologne
  • Kinderdijk
💡 Offline maps are a lifesaver! Even without mobile data, you can search addresses, get walking directions, and navigate step-by-step. Perfect backup if your eSIM has issues — and saves a lot of mobile data when you're just walking around.
