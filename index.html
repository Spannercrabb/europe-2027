Leg 1 · Zurich 🇨🇭

Zurich, Switzerland
Wed 24 – Sat 27 March 3 nights

Your gentle landing pad. Zurich is safe, calm, and gorgeous — perfect for shaking off the jetlag from the long flight. The old town (Altstadt) wraps around a sparkling blue lake, with Swiss trams, cobbled lanes, and chocolate shops on every corner.

🎂 Your birthday is Wednesday 24 March — the day you arrive in Zurich! 🎉

🏨 Our hotel — booked ✓

  • Hotel Felix · Booking.com Genius rated
  • 📍 Right in the heart of Zurich's Old Town (City Centre)
  • Check-in: Wed 24 March (from 3:00 pm)
  • Check-out: Sat 27 March (by 12:00 noon)
  • 2 rooms · 3 nights
💡 Fantastic location! Zähringerstrasse is right in the heart of Zurich's Altstadt (Old Town) — you can walk to Grossmünster, the lake, Bahnhofstrasse shopping, and countless cafés in just minutes. Zurich Main Station (for the Basel train on Saturday) is also only a short walk away. Perfect base for your birthday celebration! 🎂

Getting from the airport

  • Zurich Airport (ZRH) to city centre is easy — trains run every 10 minutes and take about 12 minutes to Zurich Hauptbahnhof (main station).
  • A taxi is around CHF 60–70.

Things to see & do

  • Altstadt (Old Town) — wander the medieval laneways, peek into Grossmünster and Fraumünster churches
  • Bahnhofstrasse — one of the world's most elegant shopping streets, perfect for window browsing
  • Lake Zurich boat trip — a short scenic ride with mountain views
  • Uetliberg Mountain — 20-minute train ride, then an easy walk to the top for panoramic Alps views
  • Lindenhof — quiet hilltop park built on Roman foundations, free lookout over the old town

🏛️ History highlights

  • Swiss National Museum — right by the station, 1000 years of Swiss history under one roof. Free audio guide in English, fabulous for a rainy afternoon. Allow 2–3 hours.
  • Grossmünster Cathedral — where the Swiss-German Reformation began in the 1500s (Huldrych Zwingli preached here). Climb the tower for epic views over the lake.
  • Fraumünster Church — famous for its Marc Chagall stained-glass windows (1970) — absolutely magical on a sunny morning. Small entry fee, worth every cent.
  • Lindenhof hill — this was the site of a Roman customs house 2,000 years ago. Now it's a peaceful park with huge chess boards and the best free view in the city.
  • St. Peter's Church — has the largest clock face in Europe (8.6 metres wide!) — right in the old town.
  • Kunsthaus Zurich — Switzerland's top art museum, with Picasso, Monet, Van Gogh, and Swiss masters. Recently expanded.

🥖 Food markets & food streets

  • Markthalle im Viadukt 🏆 — a gorgeous covered food hall in a converted railway viaduct. Artisan cheese, fresh bread, Swiss wine, charcuterie, olives, flowers. Open Mon–Sat. A must-visit!
  • Bürkliplatz Farmer's Market — right on the lake! Tuesday & Friday mornings, local produce, cheese, flowers, honey. Beautiful lakeside setting.
  • Helvetiaplatz Market — local produce market, Tuesday & Friday mornings in the trendy Kreis 4 district
  • Niederdorfstrasse — the old town's food & restaurant street, great for evening strolls with gelato or hot chocolate
  • Confiserie Sprüngli (Bahnhofstrasse) — legendary 1836 chocolate shop. Pick up Luxemburgerli (tiny macarons) and truffles. Upstairs café is a treat!
  • Schober (Napfgasse) — famous old-world café for real Swiss hot chocolate and pastries

Food & treats to try

  • Cheese fondue at a traditional restaurant — classic Swiss comfort food
  • Rösti — crispy Swiss potato pancake, often topped with eggs or cheese
  • Luxemburgerli from Confiserie Sprüngli — tiny macarons, take a box home
  • Hot chocolate at any Sprüngli or Lindt café
💡 Tip: Zurich is expensive — about 1.5× Australian prices. A simple café lunch is AUD $30–40. Use supermarkets (Coop, Migros) for cheaper snacks and picnic food.

Leg 2 · Basel 🇨🇭

Basel, Switzerland
Sat 27 – Sun 28 March 1 night

A charming overnight stop before the cruise. Basel sits right where Switzerland, Germany and France meet on the Rhine — and it's where your Viking ship is waiting! Pretty old town, art galleries, and a lovely riverside walk.

🏨 Our hotel — booked ✓

  • Hotel Rheinfelderhof ⭐⭐
  • 📍 Clara/Kleinbasel neighbourhood (close to the Rhine and Viking ship)
  • Check-in: from 3:00 pm Sat 27 March
  • Check-out: until 11:00 am Sun 28 March
💡 Location note: Hammerstrasse is in the Clara/Kleinbasel neighbourhood on the north side of the Rhine — a quiet, walkable area with easy tram access to the old town and the river. Perfect for a short stroll to the Viking ship on Sunday morning!

Getting there from Zurich

  • Direct train from Zurich HB to Basel SBB — about 1 hour, trains run every 15–30 minutes
  • Swiss trains are famously punctual, clean and comfortable — one of the trip highlights in itself
  • Tickets around CHF 35–50 one-way (book on sbb.ch)

🏛️ What to do in one afternoon — history highlights

  • Basel Minster (Münster) — 1000-year-old red sandstone cathedral where the famous scholar Erasmus of Rotterdam is buried. The Pfalz terrace behind it has the best view over the Rhine.
  • Marktplatz & the red Rathaus — the stunning 500-year-old town hall covered in frescoes and painted shields. One of the most photographed buildings in Switzerland!
  • Old Town stroll — medieval streets, painted fountains (look for the Fasnachts-Brunnen by Jean Tinguely!), flower boxes everywhere
  • Rhine ferry — cross the river the old-fashioned way on a rope-pulled ferry dating back to the 1850s. Just CHF 2 — a magical Basel tradition!
  • Spalentor gate — a massive medieval city gate from 1400, one of the most beautiful in Switzerland
  • Kunstmuseum Basel — oldest public art museum in the world (1661)! Picasso, Rembrandt, Holbein
  • Sunset walk along the Rhine promenade — perfect on a mild evening, with river swimmers in summer

🥖 Food markets

  • Markthalle Basel 🏆 — a gorgeous covered food hall in a huge domed building. Swiss, Italian, French, Asian food stalls all under one roof. Great for a quick authentic lunch! 5 mins from your hotel.
  • Marktplatz Market — daily morning market right in front of the red Rathaus. Fresh bread, cheese, fruit, flowers. Perfect for picnic supplies!
  • Läckerli Huus — try Basler Läckerli, the city's traditional spiced honey biscuits (a Basel specialty since 1720!)
💡 Tip: Try to book a hotel close to the Rhine so you can walk (or take a short taxi) to the Viking ship on Sunday morning. The Viking dock is near Schifflände or Kleinbasel depending on the ship.

The heart of the trip! You'll glide gently down the Rhine on the Viking Kara, stopping at a different riverside town each day. Castles, vineyards, medieval cities, and tulip fields — and your floating hotel comes with you.

Our cabins

Day-by-day ports of call

  1. Sun 28 Mar — Basel 🇨🇭 · Board the ship in the afternoon, settle in, welcome dinner on board
  2. Mon 29 Mar — Breisach 🇩🇪 · Gateway to Germany's Black Forest and the pretty wine village of Riquewihr in France. Cuckoo clocks, pinot noir, and fairytale forests.
  3. Tue 30 Mar — Strasbourg 🇫🇷 · A jewel of Alsace with a soaring Gothic cathedral, half-timbered houses, and canals. "Petite France" is unforgettable.

What to expect on board

  • All meals included — breakfast, lunch and dinner in the main restaurant (buffet or menu)

  • Free WiFi on board (may be slow in some stretches)

💡 Tip: The Middle Rhine stretch (between Rüdesheim and Koblenz) has over 20 castles on the hills in just 65km — the most in any river valley in the world. Don't miss this sailing day on deck!

Leg 4 · Amsterdam 🌷

Amsterdam, Netherlands
Sun 4 – Thu 8 April 4 nights

Early April is tulip season in Holland — you've timed it perfectly! Amsterdam is canals, gabled houses, bikes, museums, and some of the friendliest people in Europe (most speak excellent English).

🌷 Tulips & flowers (the best time to visit is right NOW!)

  • Keukenhof Gardens 🏆 — the world's most famous tulip gardens, only open March–May. 7 million flowers across 32 hectares in every colour imaginable. A must on a trip to Holland in April! Day trip by bus or guided tour from Amsterdam (~45 minutes).
  • Flower fields by bike or tour — the tulip fields around Lisse (near Keukenhof) explode into colour in April. You can rent bikes or join a "tulip field" tour to see them from above.
  • Bloemenmarkt (floating flower market) — the world's only floating flower market, right in central Amsterdam on the Singel canal. Hundreds of fresh tulip bouquets and bulbs to take home (get the certified "export-ready" bulbs so customs won't confiscate them!)
  • Tulip Museum (Amsterdam Tulip Museum) — small but lovely, tells the whole history of "tulip mania" in the 1600s when a single bulb cost more than a house!
  • Hortus Botanicus — beautiful 1638 botanical gardens in the heart of Amsterdam, one of the oldest in the world. Stunning tulip displays in April.
  • FloraHolland Aalsmeer flower auction — the world's biggest flower trading floor. Go early (arrive by 7 am!) to see millions of flowers changing hands in real time. Unforgettable if you love flowers.

🏛️ History & culture

  • Anne Frank House — deeply moving, a must-visit. Book online at least 6 weeks in advance — they sell out fast and there's no ticket booth on the day.
  • Rijksmuseum — the big one! Vermeer's Milkmaid, Rembrandt's Night Watch, Dutch Golden Age masters. In a stunning 1885 building — allow 3 hours.
  • Van Gogh Museum — the world's largest Van Gogh collection (200+ paintings). Incredible to see the colours in real life. Pre-book tickets a few weeks ahead.
  • Canal cruise — a gentle 1-hour boat tour is the loveliest way to see the city. Book a "small boat" tour (max 12 people) for a better experience than the big glass boats.
  • Begijnhof — hidden medieval courtyard from the 1300s, where Catholic women lived quietly. Totally peaceful and free to visit.
  • Dam Square & Royal Palace — the heart of Amsterdam, with the 17th-century palace where Dutch royals still occasionally live
  • Westerkerk — the tallest church tower in Amsterdam, next to Anne Frank House. You can climb it for city views (mild stairs!)
  • Jordaan neighbourhood — pretty canals, little cafés, vintage shops. The most photogenic area in the city.
  • Vondelpark — lovely park for a relaxed afternoon walk under blossom trees in April

🥖 Food markets (Amsterdam has brilliant ones!)

  • Albert Cuyp Market 🏆 — Amsterdam's biggest & most famous street market, open Mon–Sat, 9 am–5 pm. Cheese, herring, fresh stroopwafels (get them warm!), flowers, clothing. Go hungry!
  • Foodhallen — an incredible food hall in a converted tram depot. 30+ gourmet food stalls (Dutch, Asian, Mexican, Italian) — perfect for a relaxed dinner where everyone can choose something different.
  • Noordermarkt — Saturday organic farmers' market in the Jordaan. Beautiful fresh produce, Dutch cheese wheels, and a famous apple pie stall (opposite Winkel 43 café!)
  • De Kaaskamer (Runstraat) — legendary cheese shop with 400+ Dutch cheeses. Ask for samples!
  • Haarlemmerstraat & Haarlemmerdijk — the "it" food street in Amsterdam: bakeries, specialty food shops, little cafés

Day trip ideas

  • Zaanse Schans — picture-book Dutch windmill village, just 30 min by train
  • Delft & The Hague — blue pottery town + Dutch royal city, doable in a day
  • Volendam & Marken — quaint fishing villages on the IJsselmeer

Food to try

  • Stroopwafels — warm syrup waffles from a street stall
  • Herring — the Dutch eat it raw with pickles and onion (brave!)
  • Bitterballen — crispy deep-fried meat snacks at any pub
  • Apple pie at Winkel 43 in the Jordaan — legendary
💡 Tip: Amsterdam is flat and tiny — walking between sights is easy. Watch out for bicycles though! They have right of way and go fast. Also: book Anne Frank House, Van Gogh, and Keukenhof tickets before you leave Australia.

🗼 The classics

  • Eiffel Tower — pre-book tickets to skip the queue. Twinkles for 5 minutes every hour after dark. Your hotel is 10 mins walk away!
  • Seine river cruise — 1-hour evening cruise with the city lit up = unforgettable
  • Tuileries & Luxembourg Gardens — springtime in Paris parks is pure joy, blossom trees in April
  • Galeries Lafayette — grand department store with a stunning stained-glass dome and a free rooftop view over Paris
  • Montmartre & Sacré-Cœur — hilltop village feel, artists' square, the famous white-domed basilica (free to enter)

🏛️ History highlights (Paris is a city of history!)

  • Louvre Museum 🏆 — the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and 35,000 treasures. Go early (9 am) or late afternoon to avoid crowds. Pre-book a timed entry. Don't try to see everything — pick 2–3 wings.
  • Notre-Dame Cathedral — reopened in December 2024 after the 2019 fire restoration. Free to enter. Gorgeous from the outside and absolutely stunning inside with its restored ceilings.
  • Sainte-Chapelle — the most beautiful stained-glass chapel in the world (1248). 15 massive stained-glass windows telling the whole Bible. Tucked on Île de la Cité — pre-book to skip the queue.
  • Musée d'Orsay — Monet, Renoir, Degas, Van Gogh, Cézanne. In a breathtaking former railway station from 1900. Many people prefer this to the Louvre!
  • Palace of Versailles — 45 minutes by train from central Paris. The Hall of Mirrors, Marie Antoinette's hamlet, vast gardens. Allow a full day and pre-book tickets!
  • Musée Carnavalet — the history of Paris itself, free entry! Room after room of Paris history from Roman times to today. A local gem.
  • Panthéon — final resting place of Voltaire, Rousseau, Marie Curie, Victor Hugo. Stunning Latin Quarter monument.
  • Conciergerie — the medieval palace that became a prison during the French Revolution. Marie Antoinette was held here before her execution. Genuinely moving.
  • Arc de Triomphe — climb the 284 steps for a view straight down the Champs-Élysées
  • Musée de l'Orangerie — Monet's huge Water Lilies in oval rooms. Small, quiet, and breathtaking.

🥖 Food markets & food streets (Paris is heaven for this!)

  • Rue Cler 🏆 — the most beautiful market street in Paris, and it's literally around the corner from your hotel! Cheese shops, bakeries, fishmongers, chocolatiers, wine shops, a florist. Grab a baguette, cheese, ham, and a bottle of wine for a picnic under the Eiffel Tower. ✨
  • Marché Bastille — Paris's biggest open-air market, Thursday & Sunday mornings. Dozens of stalls, cheese wheels the size of tyres, rotisserie chickens, oysters being shucked. A proper Parisian experience!
  • Marché des Enfants Rouges — the OLDEST covered market in Paris (1615!). In the Marais. Small but gorgeous — grab lunch from one of the food stalls (Moroccan, Italian, Japanese, French).
  • Rue Mouffetard — a medieval food market street in the Latin Quarter. Cobblestones, flower shops, fromageries, bakeries, tiny restaurants. A morning stroll here is a joy.
  • Marché d'Aligre — cheap, lively, local — not touristy at all. Combined open-air and indoor market in the 12th.
  • Galeries Lafayette Gourmet — the food hall section of the famous department store. French patisserie, cheese, wine, chocolate, ready meals to take back to the hotel.
  • La Grande Épicerie de Paris — the legendary food hall inside Le Bon Marché, considered the best in Paris. Worth a visit just to browse the gorgeous displays.

Day trip ideas

  • Palace of Versailles — easy 45-min train from central Paris. Go early, allow a full day.
  • Giverny — Monet's gardens (only open April–October). Breathtaking in spring.
  • Reims & Champagne region — under an hour on the TGV for cathedral + champagne tastings

Food to try

  • Croissant & café crème at a corner bakery — breakfast, every day
  • Macarons from Ladurée or Pierre Hermé
  • Steak-frites at a classic brasserie
  • Crêpes from a street stand (sweet or savoury)
  • Fresh baguette & cheese picnic in the Tuileries — a Parisian classic
💡 Tip: Paris Metro is quick and cheap — buy a pack of 10 tickets ("carnet") or a Navigo Easy card. Watch for pickpockets on busy lines (especially near the Eiffel Tower and at Gare du Nord) — keep handbags zipped and close to you.

📘 Visas & Travel Authorisation

What you need as an Australian
Apply: early 2027

Good news: Australians do not need a traditional visa for any of the countries on your trip! Switzerland, Germany, the Netherlands, and France are all in the Schengen Area, where Australians can visit visa-free for up to 90 days.

🆕 ETIAS — the new travel authorisation

From late 2026, Europe is bringing in a new system called ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorisation System). It's not a visa — it's a quick online authorisation like the US ESTA. By March 2027, it's very likely to be required.

DetailInfo
Cost€20 per person (≈ AUD $33)
Free forUnder 18 or over 70 years old (none of you qualify)
Validity3 years, or until your passport expires
CoversAll Schengen countries (1 ETIAS = Switzerland + Germany + Netherlands + France, plus 25 others)
How long to approveUsually minutes, can take up to 96 hours
Launch dateExpected Q4 2026 (October–December 2026)

🌐 Official website — ONLY use this one!

⚠️ Watch out for scam sites! Many fake websites charge $60–$100 for ETIAS. The only official site is:

🔗 travel-europe.europa.eu/etias_en

Look for a URL ending in .europa.eu — anything else is NOT official.

📋 What you'll need to apply

  • Valid Australian passport (with at least 3 months validity beyond your return date)
  • Email address
  • Debit or credit card for the €20 fee
  • Personal details (name, DOB, passport number)
  • Your first Schengen country of entry (that's Switzerland for you)

⏰ When to apply

  • As soon as ETIAS opens (expected late 2026)
  • Ideally by January or February 2027 — at least 4–6 weeks before flying
  • Don't leave it to the last minute! Approval is usually quick, but if there's any issue it can take up to 96 hours, and you cannot board your flight without it once the system is live

📘 Passport check — do this NOW

⭐ Important: Your passport must be valid for at least 3 months after 13 April 2027 — so valid until at least July 2027. If it expires before then, renew it now! Australian passport renewals can take 6+ weeks during busy periods. Apply at passports.gov.au.

🛂 Other things to know

  • EES (Entry/Exit System): A new biometric border system launched in 2025 — no application, just fingerprints/photo at the airport on arrival. Nothing to do beforehand.
  • 90-day rule: You can stay up to 90 days in any 180-day period across all Schengen countries combined — your 21-day trip is well within this
  • Switzerland is Schengen too (even though it's not in the EU) — you don't need anything extra for it

Handy tips to stretch the budget

  • 🇨🇭 Switzerland is the pricey one — a simple café lunch can be AUD $40. Use supermarkets (Coop or Migros) for lunches, save restaurants for dinner
  • 🥐 Paris & Amsterdam: Grab croissants and coffee from bakeries (€4–6) instead of hotel breakfast buffets (€15–25)
  • 🍷 House wine at restaurants is almost always great, and half the price of named bottles
  • 🥪 Picnic lunches in parks are cheaper AND more fun — French bread, cheese, and fruit under the Eiffel Tower is a memory for life
  • 💧 Tap water is free and safe everywhere — ask for "l'eau du robinet" in France, "Leitungswasser" in Switzerland/Germany, "kraanwater" in the Netherlands
  • 🍽️ Lunch menus at nice restaurants are often half the price of dinner — same food, better deal!
  • 🎫 Fixed-price "menu du jour" (menu of the day) is usually the best value in French bistros

⏰ When to order cards & get cash

  • 3–4 weeks before departure: Apply for Wise and ING cards online — they take 1–2 weeks to arrive
  • 2 weeks before: Activate cards, test them at an Aussie ATM, top up Wise with some Euros
  • 1 week before: Order cash from Travelex, Australia Post, or your bank (order online for better rates than walk-in)
  • 🔗 travelex.com.au (free home delivery or pick up at airport)
  • ⚠️ NEVER exchange cash at the airport on arrival — the rates are terrible!

🚨 Critical tip: ALWAYS pay in local currency!

⚠️ Watch out for "DCC" (Dynamic Currency Conversion): When you tap your card at a European shop or ATM, the machine might ask "Pay in AUD or EUR?" — ALWAYS choose EUR (or CHF in Switzerland)!

If you pick "AUD", the shop does the conversion at a terrible rate and you can lose 5–8% extra on every transaction. Over 3 weeks this adds up to hundreds of dollars. Just always pay in the local currency and let your Wise/ING card handle it — they'll give you a much better rate.

Other money tips

  • 📞 Tell your banks you're travelling before you go — otherwise they may block your cards thinking there's fraud
  • 📱 Download banking apps and make sure you remember your passwords
  • 🏧 Use bank ATMs (e.g. UBS, Credit Suisse, ING, BNP Paribas) — avoid "Euronet" and other "Travelex" coloured ATMs which charge huge fees
  • 💸 Withdraw larger amounts less often — ATM fees are usually a flat fee, so one €200 withdrawal is cheaper than four €50 ones
  • 💳 Keep cards in separate bags — if one handbag is stolen, you still have a card
